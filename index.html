What is Janus-Q?

Janus-Q is an innovative end-to-end event trading investment platform that leverages advanced Large Language Models (LLMs) to analyze financial events and make intelligent trading decisions. The platform processes various event types including personal behavior, equity changes, asset changes, dividends, risk warnings, financing, financial status updates, violations, industry trends, and rating adjustments.

Our comprehensive backtesting framework evaluates the trading capability of LLMs across various financial markets, providing a unified environment for ingesting external information, driving multi-agent systems, and making data-driven trading decisions.

Performance Metrics Explanation

TR (Total Return)

Total cumulative return over the entire backtesting period.

SR (Sharpe Ratio)

Risk-adjusted return metric. Higher values indicate better risk-adjusted performance.

MDD (Maximum Drawdown)

Largest peak-to-trough decline. Lower values indicate less risk.

DA (Direction Accuracy)

Accuracy of predicting price movement direction. Higher is better.

ETA (Event Type Accuracy)

Accuracy of classifying financial events into correct types. Higher is better.

About Janus-Q

Our Research and Platform

Project Overview

Janus-Q is a cutting-edge research project that explores the intersection of Large Language Models (LLMs) and quantitative finance. Our platform provides an end-to-end framework for event-driven trading strategies, leveraging the power of AI to process and analyze financial events in real-time.

The platform evaluates various LLM models across multiple dimensions including Annual Return Rate (ARR), Sharpe Ratio (SR), Maximum Drawdown (MDD), and Calmar Ratio (CR), providing comprehensive insights into model performance.

Janus-Q Framework

Overview of the two-stage fine-tuning framework for event-driven trading: Stage~I establishes a reasoning-aware Event-to-CAR mapping via supervised fine-tuning on news, market signals, and firm attributes; Stage~II introduces a Hierarchical Gated Reward Model (HGRM) for reinforcement fine-tuning.

Platform Contributions

üìä Leaderboard

A comprehensive leaderboard showcasing the performance of different LLM agents for event-driven trading in a transparent arena view. Compare models across multiple performance metrics and event types.

üìö Dataset

We construct a large-scale dataset of 62,400 financial news articles with manual annotations covering 10 event types, associated stocks, and event-driven abnormal returns, providing a unified benchmark for systematic event-level market impact analysis.

üß™ Simulated Environment

A simulated trading environment allowing for rigorous performance evaluation across various dimensions, including different event types, time periods, and market conditions. This enables systematic testing and comparison of event-driven trading strategies.
